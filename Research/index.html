From theoretical point of view, our research focuses on: \begin{enumerate} \item Data driven motion planning and control (presented in AAAI~\cite{sanni2022deep} and RSS~\cite{willow2022} papers, and IROS/RAL~\cite{Alessandra2022} and IROS 2022~\cite{giorgo2022} submissions) \item Robot learning from demonstrations \cite{ghalamzan2015incremental, ghalamzan2018, osa2017guiding,Alessandra2022, sanni2022deep, giorgo2022} \item Haptic enabled teleoperation \cite{ghalamzan2017human, selvaggio2019haptic, parsa2022impact} and ~\cite{parsa2020haptic}--\textbf{Best Paper Award} Finalist; \item Grasping and manipulation planning/control \cite{mavrakis2017safe, mavrakis2016task,pardi2018choosing, pardi2021optimal} and \cite{mavrakis2016analysis}-- \textbf{Best Interactive Paper Award} Finalist at Humanoids 2016) \item Robot Perception~\cite{Alessandra2021} \end{enumerate} \noindent \textbf{Robot Learning from Demonstrations (LfD):} is key to bring robots to human workspaces at scale. It also can help reduce programming time and cost so that non-experts can easily programme a robot for different tasks. The links between robot and human LfD \cite{ghalamzan2015incremental, ghalamzan2018} demonstrates the available technologies are far away from the corresponding human capabilities of learning from observations. We have been working on developing LfD approaches for a wide range of applications, e.g. Breast Cancer Examination robot~\cite{sanni2022deep}, strawberry picking~\cite{Alessandra2022}, simple pick-and-place~\cite{ghalamzan2015incremental, ghalamzan2018} and nuclear sort and segregation~\cite{osa2017guiding}. We am keen to develop fundamental concepts to make LfD available for non-expert users across several application domains. \noindent \textbf{Data-driven predictive models} Most manipulation tasks involve interaction with environments. These interactions are always very hard to model via classical approaches, hence, very challenging to control. We am interested in developing data-driven predictive forward models that can help a robot to interact with its environment. For instance, \cite{nazari2021tactile} and \cite{willow2022} presents \textit{action conditioned predictive model}s for tactile sensors. These methods can be used for, e.g., robust object manipulation by slip avoidance controller via model predictive control (MPC). It can be also used for cluster manipulation for picking up an object on the shelf or strawberry picking~\cite{mghames2020interactive}. Predictive models are a core part of a robust interactive robotic task. \noindent \textbf{Reduced cognitive load teleoperation} fully teleoperated robotic systems are sole solutions to many problems, e.g. from robotic surgery to nuclear sort \& segregation. These conservative industries do not trust a fully autonomous system even if a such system is available and need a human operator to be in charge of the task execution. We worked on a new technology of shared control which can bridge the gap between the autonomous systems and fully teleoperated systems. We proposed to use haptic force cues as a means to provide guidance for human operator. The guidance in teleoperation can provide a human operator with optimal actions in using a surgical robot~\cite{selvaggio2019haptic} or predicted singularities~\cite{ghalamzan2017human}, and collision~\cite{parsa2020haptic} and minimum required torques~\cite{Rahal2022}. Not only haptic-guidance is important in reducing the cognitive workload~\cite{Talha2016}. Motion scaling is also an important factor for reducing the cognitive workload~\cite{Talha2016}. We envisage an Intelligent Tele-operation System benefiting from Intelligent Autonomous System via haptic-guidance or adaptive motion scaling. That can increase the penetration of robotic technologies in the market and make them cheaper solutions, e.g. it can significantly reduce the operation time of robotic surgery. \noindent \textbf{Robotic grasping and manipulation} My work in grasping and manipulation is manifold: (1) task-informed grasping (TIG); (2) Motion planning; (3) Motion Control. The common practice of robotic grasping include an intelligent system extracting grasp related features from RGB-D images. This alone is not enough for effective and robust robotic grasping as the synthesised grasp may not be good enough for a specific manipulator. Task-informed grasping considers robot specific objectives into consideration in selecting the grasp poses. Hence, the robot can predict the consequences of the grasp actions (e.g. singularity~\cite{GhalamzanE2016}, torques~\cite{mavrakis2016analysis}, torques~\cite{mavrakis2017safe}, collision~\cite{pardi2018choosing} and affordance~\cite{pardi2021optimal}) during the planned manipulative movements. predicted , collision~\cite{pardi2018choosing}, required torque~\cite{pardi2021optimal} of a follower manipulator % For interactive robotic manipulation motion planning and control are needed. For effective motion planning for robotic cutting one should define the robot movements based on the surface of the object to be cut~\cite{pardi2020planning}. For robotic breast palpation (to localise the cancerous lumps) motion control and searching policy, such as RL, are required~\cite{giorgo2022}.
